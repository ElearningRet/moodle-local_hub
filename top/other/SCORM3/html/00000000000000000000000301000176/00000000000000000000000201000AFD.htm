1970s-1980s: LANs and WANs

In the 1970s and 1980s, Local-area networks (LANs), minicomputers, and shared wide-area networks (WANs) evolved. Minicomputers, often located away from the central data center, facilitated the emergence of distributed data processing.

LANs allowed for multimedia-based e-learning to be used because of its greater bandwidth capabilities.

Interconnected Networks
LANs evolved primarily to enable sharing of expensive resources. The potential strategic importance of interconnected networks was quickly realized. These networks provided the basis for enterprise-wide applications such as e-mail and file transfer.
 

Impact on E-Learning
Learners in the local area can all connect to a common server that can deliver and track their learning progress. However, delivery and tracking are limited to the LANs, unless the network is connected to a larger WAN. LANs also allow for higher bandwidth training such as multimedia and streaming video.
How did LANs affect e-learning?
They opened up e-learning to a global audience.
Learners accessed the training content from a mainframe network.
The higher bandwidth opened up the opportunity for streaming video and multimedia.
