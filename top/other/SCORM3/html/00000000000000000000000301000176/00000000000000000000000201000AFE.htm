1980-2000s: Internetworking & the Internet

In the 1980s and 1990s, networks were a mixture of old and new technologies. Local networks, public data networks, leased lines, and high-speed mainframe channels have all been used on an opportunistic basis with little regard for overall integration and consistency.

The approach to computer communication in most organizations had to grow rapidly in response to new technologies, the evolution of business requirements, and the need for "instant" knowledge transfer. In turn, this created the need for flexible, scalable, and adaptable internetworks.

Internetworks
Internetworks tie together LANs and WANs, computer systems, software, and a variety of different devices to form the corporate communication infrastructure.
 

Impact on E-Learning
Learners in geographically dispersed areas can connect to the same e-learning experience. Unlike a privately operated WAN, internetworking and the Internet allow for learners to find and access the same e-learning event. This allows companies to offer e-learning to others, and then deliver and track their progress.

The Internet, however, does have some limitations for the learner. The capabilities of the learner's computer, the bandwidth of the network connection, and language differences are three of many issues that impact e-learning through internetworks.
