Overview
Computer networks have enabled e-learning for students worldwide. Starting with the corporate-owned computer systems of the '60s, through today's global Internet, each networking architecture has opened up new possibilities for e-learning. Today training has moved from traditional classrooms to computer-enabled, networked, interactive, multimedia, anytime, anywhere experiences.
Objectives
Upon completing this lesson, you will be able to:
 
  • Select a scenario that best shows how LANs are being used in e-learning

  • Select a scenario that best shows how WANs work to support e-learning

  • Summarize how internetworks evolved

Importance
Recognizing the types of networks used for computing is important because of how they are used for e-learning. Each network has benefits and limitations that may impact the type of e-learning experience you can offer a student.
Prerequisites
No prior knowledge about networks is required. It is helpful if you have a basic understanding of the World Wide Web and the concept of e-learning.
 

Scenario
ABC Company, a small startup has traditionally delivered five-day classroom-based training at its corporate headquarters. While this was fine for their local students, the company has recently merged with a global organization, ZZX, Inc.

The challenge for Corissa, ABC's training director, is to deliver all those five-day training courses to ZZX's global audience. Corissa is familiar with the World Wide Web, and wants to use e-learning to solve her business need.

Her first step is to research the types of networks, and determine out how they are used in e-learning.
